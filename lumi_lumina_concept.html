Mascot Concept · Alternative 3

The Lumina

Not a creature that has a glow — it is the glow. A borderless, abstract spirit of light that communicates entirely through colour, scale, and motion. Simplicity as the ultimate sophistication, taken to its logical conclusion.

Character Identity

Lumina strips away biological taxonomy entirely. No animal, no species, no legs or wings or antennae. It is pure light with just enough personality — two dot eyes and a whisper of a smile — to make it feel like a companion rather than an icon.

Inspired by the Headspace approach to abstract characters, paper lanterns, and the will-o'-the-wisp. The form has no hard borders or stroke outlines. It's defined entirely by the contrast between its radial gradients and the background.

Core principle: The mascot doesn't have a glow. It is the glow. The entire entity is a soft, shifting focal point of light — warm, present, and completely unobtrusive.

Pure Light Minimal Serene Universal Modern
"Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication."

This is the only concept that takes that principle to its logical conclusion. By removing form — no body, no limbs, no silhouette to decode — the mascot becomes pure communication. It speaks entirely through colour, scale, density, and motion. It's the closest a mascot can get to being invisible while still being deeply present.

Design Approach

Three layers of intentional reduction

No Borders

Form defined entirely by gradient contrast against background. No outlines, no strokes.

No Anatomy

No limbs, wings, or features to trigger aversion. Universally safe and cross-culturally neutral.

Pure Communication

States expressed entirely through colour shift, scale change, and glow density. Maximum signal, zero noise.

Mood States

Colour, scale, and glow density carry all the emotional information — no morphological changes needed

Default State

Happy

Warm amber glow, steady breathing scale animation (1.0→1.03 over 4.5s). Soft smile visible. The companion at rest.

Milestone / Streak

Celebrating

Glow expands dramatically, shifts to rose-pink. Orb pulses larger with each beat. Star eyes appear. Light motes burst outward.

Nudge / Motivation

Encouraging

Glow shifts to diffuse mint green. Gentle upward float animation. Eyes widen slightly, wider smile. Halo spreads outward.

Loading / Processing

Thinking

Glow tightens to sky-blue with hard bright centre. Rhythmic radial pulse expands/contracts. One eye squints. Thought dots drift.

Reading Log Flow

Reading

Shrinks down, eyes half-closed in contentment. Glow dims to 15-30% opacity — a soft reading lamp. Completely non-intrusive.

Inactive / Empty State

Sleeping

Glow fades almost entirely to muted grey. Orb deflates slightly. Eyes closed. Slow breathing. The ember of a reading lamp turned low.

Onboarding / Welcome

Waving

Glow pulsates amber→warm orange. A lateral light expansion mimics a greeting gesture. No limbs needed — light itself waves.

Design Rationale

Why abstract over animal? The research report argues convincingly that biological taxonomy introduces risk — insect aversion, owl clichés, fox confusion with Mozilla. Lumina sidesteps all of it. There's nothing to trigger, nothing to compare it to, nothing that dates. It's a shape and a colour, and that's intentionally everything.

Why borderless? Your existing design system already communicates through soft shadows, gradient fills, and rounded containers rather than hard outlines. Lumina takes this to its extreme — the mascot has zero stroke outlines. Its form is defined entirely by the radial gradient against the background. It feels like a native part of your UI rather than a sticker placed on top.

Why is this adult-appropriate? This is the Headspace model. Abstract, warm, slightly ambiguous shapes register as sophisticated because they resist categorisation. Adults don't need a cute animal to feel engaged — they respond to elegance, restraint, and intentionality. Lumina respects the reader's intelligence by not trying to charm them with a character. It simply provides warmth.

The performance argument: Lumina is the lightest of all three concepts in terms of frontend rendering. No skeletal SVG rigging, no complex path animations. It's circles, radial gradients, and CSS scale/opacity transitions — all hardware-accelerated. This makes it the most performant option for your Flutter app.

The risk: Minimal personality. The dot eyes and faint smile give it enough character for functional UX moments, but it may lack the narrative depth and "story you can tell" that the Revontulet concept offers. It won't generate the same social media engagement or brand storytelling opportunities. It's the purist's choice — perfectly refined, but intentionally quiet.
