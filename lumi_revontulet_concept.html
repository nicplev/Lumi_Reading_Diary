The Revontulet

An arctic fox spirit from Finnish folklore — the creature whose tail sweeps sparks of light across the snow, creating the Aurora Borealis. Snow meets light. Lumi in both meanings.

Character Identity

In Finnish mythology, the Aurora Borealis is called "revontulet" — literally "fox fires." The story tells of an arctic fox whose tail sweeps across the snowdrifts, sending sparks of light into the night sky.

This mascot bridges both meanings of Lumi: the Finnish word for snow and the Latin root for light. The fox is rendered as a smooth, borderless spirit — no sharp teeth, no claws, no jagged fur. Just sweeping curves and a luminous tail.

Core quirk: The tail is the emotional engine. It shifts colour through your existing palette — amber at rest, rose-pink when celebrating, mint-green when encouraging. Aurora sparks drift upward from its tip.

The Dual Etymology of Lumi

The name "Lumi" uniquely bridges two languages and two powerful metaphors — creating a brand identity that's layered, culturally rich, and entirely ownable.

The Revontulet concept is the only mascot direction that unifies both meanings simultaneously: an arctic creature (snow) whose tail creates light (illumination). Reading as the aurora of the mind.

Design Rationale

Why an arctic fox over a firefly? The research report's core argument is sound: insect morphology — segmented bodies, angular wings, antennae — runs counter to the soft, continuous curves that define your entire UI system (Nunito font, pill-shaped buttons, 20pt border-radius cards). The fox's silhouette is naturally composed of sweeping organic curves. No segmentation, no sharp joints.

Why the glowing tail specifically? It centralises the "light" metaphor into a single, iconic design element. The firefly distributed its glow across the abdomen — less distinctive at small sizes. The fox's tail is asymmetric and immediately recognisable. At 24×24px, the silhouette reads as: round body + one luminous sweep to the left. No other major app mascot shares this profile.

Why the Finnish mythology? It gives you a story to tell. "Lumi's mascot is an arctic fox from Finnish folklore — the creature that creates the Northern Lights by sweeping its tail across the snow." That's a sentence that sticks. It elevates the brand from "reading app with a cute animal" to "reading app with a genuine cultural identity." And it's the only concept that unifies both etymologies of your name simultaneously.

Why aurora sparks as the particle system? Your firefly concept used confetti and sparkles. The fox's aurora sparks (green/purple/blue) are more distinctive and directly tied to the mythology. They also give you a secondary colour accent palette (aurora green, aurora purple, aurora blue) that enriches your design system without conflicting with existing colours.
