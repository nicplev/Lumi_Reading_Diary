## Executive Summary

This research report provides a comprehensive analysis to inform the development of Lumi, a **digital reading diary and tracking application**. Lumi is designed to replace physical paper reading diaries used in Australian schools, providing a streamlined way for parents to log nightly reading sessions and for teachers to track student progress.

**Important Clarification:** Lumi is NOT a digital reading platform. Physical books remain central to the reading experience. Teachers assign physical decodable and library books to students at school, and students take these books home to read with their parents. Lumi simply tracks and records this reading activity - it does not host, display, or provide digital versions of the books themselves.

Key findings reveal that physical reading diaries serve critical functions including distinguishing between library books (read TO child) and decodable books (child reads TO parent), tracking reading frequency with milestone-based motivation, facilitating parent-teacher communication, and enabling child self-assessment through simple visual systems like traffic lights and emoji faces.

The digital transformation of these diaries offers significant opportunities to reduce friction through touch-only interfaces, teacher-managed book assignment lists, pre-written comment templates, and gamification elements that drive consistent reading habits while maintaining the educational integrity of the decodable reading approach.

## Section 1: Lumi Product Definition

### 1.1 What Lumi IS

- A digital reading diary that replaces paper-based home reading journals
- A tracking and analytics system for monitoring student reading progress
- A communication tool between parents and teachers regarding reading
- A motivation system with streaks, milestones, and digital rewards
- A book assignment management tool for teachers

### 1.2 What Lumi is NOT

- NOT a digital reading platform (no e-books or digital book content)
- NOT a replacement for physical books (students still read physical books)
- NOT a reading instruction app (no phonics lessons or reading activities)
- NOT a book discovery or recommendation engine

### 1.3 Core Workflow

The Lumi workflow mirrors existing school practices:

1. **Teacher Assigns Books:** At school, the teacher selects physical books for each student and assigns them via Lumi (ISBN scanning makes this quick)
2. **Student Takes Books Home:** The child physically carries their assigned books home in their school bag
3. **Parent Logs Reading:** At home, parent opens Lumi, selects from the child's short list of assigned books, and logs the reading session with one-tap interactions
4. **Teacher Reviews Progress:** Teacher views class dashboard showing which students read, streaks, and any parent comments
5. **Cycle Repeats:** When books are returned, teacher assigns new books and the cycle continues

## Section 2: Physical Reading Diary Analysis

Analysis of 25 images from multiple physical reading diary products revealed consistent patterns across different publishers including Learning Logic Pty Ltd (Little Learners Love Literacy), Kluwell, and other Australian educational publishers.

### 2.1 Diary Products Analyzed

Three distinct reading diary formats were examined, each targeting Lower Primary (Foundation/Prep to Year 2) students:

| Publisher | Product Name | Key Feature |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| Learning Logic Pty Ltd | Home Reading Journal | Library/Decodable distinction with traffic light assessment |
| Take-Home Reading Journal | Lower Primary Edition | Star rating system, milestone tree visualization |
| Kluwell | My Home Reading (Yellow Level) | Emoji faces for self-assessment, weekly comments |
| I Love Reading Books | Take-Home Reading Diary | Sticker rewards, favourite character section |

### 2.2 Core Data Fields Identified

All physical diaries consistently captured the following essential data points:

#### Reading Session Data

- **Night/Session Number:** Running count of total reading nights (e.g., Night 21, 22, 23...)
- **Date:** Day of week with date format (Mon /, Tues /, Wed /)
- **Book Title:** Title of book read (in Lumi: selected from assigned book list)
- **Book Type:** Critical distinction - Library Book OR Decodable Book
- **Parent Initials:** Verification that parent was present during reading

#### Assessment Fields

- **How Did It Go? / How Did You Feel?:** Child self-assessment using traffic light colors (green=confident, orange=little challenging, red=challenging) OR 1-4 star rating OR emoji faces
- **Carer Comment:** Free-text area for parent/carer to write observations about the reading session
- **Teacher Comment:** Dedicated space for teacher feedback and communication

#### Engagement & Motivation Elements

- **Sticker Box:** Space for reward stickers placed by teacher or parent
- **Favourite Character:** Child can note their favourite character from books read
- **Weekend/Holiday Reading:** Optional section for additional reading notes

### 2.3 Milestone & Reward Systems

A critical motivational component across all diaries was the milestone tracking system. The Learning Logic diary uses a tree house visualization where children color in numbers 1-20 to climb to the tree house, with checkpoints at 50, 100, and 200 nights. Awards are given every 25 nights of reading, creating 8 achievement milestones throughout the school year.

This gamification approach aligns with research showing that visual progress tracking and achievable incremental goals drive intrinsic motivation in children. The physical sticker system provides tangible rewards that children can see and touch - Lumi's digital badges must create equivalent satisfaction.

### 2.4 Parent Education Content

Physical diaries include substantial parent education sections explaining:

- **The "When Stuck on a Word" Strategy:** A 5-step visual process - (1) Look at the word, (2) Sound out tracking with finger, (3) Blend sounds together, (4) Say the word, (5) Discuss meaning and re-read
- **Reading TO vs. WITH Child:** Clear explanation that library books are read TO the child while decodable books are read BY the child to the parent
- **Top Tips for Parents:** Before reading warm-up, tracking with finger, pausing at punctuation, repeated reading for fluency
- **Comment Examples:** Pre-written suggestions like "Lexie's expression was fantastic" and "We discussed 'to' and 'too' after reading this book"

## Section 3: Decodable Reading & Science of Reading

### 3.1 What is Decodable Reading?

Decodable reading is an approach where early readers practice with texts containing a high percentage of words they can "sound out" using phonics principles they have already been explicitly taught. According to reading research, decodable books should contain at least 64% or more words identifiable using taught phonics elements.

This approach differs fundamentally from leveled readers which rely on predictability, picture cues, and memorization. When children use letter-sounds to decode words, they engage in orthographic mapping - the brain process that stores words for automatic recognition.

### 3.2 The Five Pillars of Reading Instruction

The National Reading Panel identified five critical areas for reading instruction:

| Pillar | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Phonemic Awareness** | The ability to hear, identify, and manipulate the approximately 44 phonemes (smallest units of sound) in English |
| **Phonics** | Understanding the relationships between letters (graphemes) and sounds (phonemes) |
| **Fluency** | Reading with speed, accuracy, and proper expression |
| **Vocabulary** | Knowledge of words and their meanings |
| **Comprehension** | Understanding and interpreting text |

### 3.3 Library Books vs. Decodable Books

This distinction is critical for Lumi's design and is emphasized in all physical diaries:

| Aspect | Library Books | Decodable Books |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Direction** | Parent reads TO child | Child reads TO parent |
| **Purpose** | Build vocabulary, comprehension, love of reading | Practice decoding skills and phonics |
| **Word Selection** | Rich vocabulary, complex patterns | Only taught phonics patterns |
| **Physical Book** | School library or home collection | Assigned by teacher, matched to phonics stage |

### 3.4 Australian Curriculum Alignment

The Australian Curriculum Version 9.0 now includes specific elements for phonological awareness, phonic knowledge and word recognition, fluency, and understanding texts. NSW has mandated phonics screening checks since 2021 and purchased decodable readers from Little Learners Love Literacy and Decodable Readers Australia for all public schools.

The Grattan Institute's 2024 report noted that one-third of Australian students are not effective, independent readers, driving increased policy focus on systematic synthetic phonics instruction.

## Section 4: User Workflows & Interfaces

### 4.1 Teacher Workflow

**Key Insight:** ISBN barcode scanning is most valuable for TEACHERS, not parents. Teachers need to quickly assign multiple books to multiple students. Scanning is faster than searching/typing when processing a classroom set of books.

#### Book Assignment Process

1. Teacher opens Lumi on tablet/phone and selects "Assign Books"
2. Teacher scans ISBN barcode on physical book (auto-populates title, author, cover image, book type)
3. Teacher selects student(s) from class list to assign book to
4. Book appears in student's "My Books" list in parent app
5. When book is returned, teacher marks as "returned" and assigns new book

#### Teacher Dashboard Features

- Class overview showing which students logged reading last night
- Streak tracking across all students (identify who needs encouragement)
- Book assignment management (who has which books)
- Parent comment review and quick-reply templates
- Milestone celebration alerts ("Sarah reached 50 nights!")

### 4.2 Parent Workflow

**Key Insight:** Parents do NOT need to scan barcodes. Children typically come home with only 2-4 assigned books at a time. It's faster for parents to tap the correct book from a short visual list than to find and scan a barcode.

#### Nightly Reading Log Process (Target: Under 30 Seconds)

1. Parent opens app and taps "Log Tonight's Reading"
2. Sees short list of assigned books with cover images - taps the book read tonight
3. Book type (Library/Decodable) is already set by teacher - parent confirms or adjusts
4. Child taps emoji face to show how reading felt
5. Parent optionally taps pre-written comment phrases
6. Parent taps "I read with my child" to confirm and save
7. **Celebration:** Streak updates, confetti animation, badge award if milestone reached

#### Parent App Features

- "My Books" list showing all currently assigned books with cover images
- One-tap book selection (no typing required)
- Pre-written comment templates (tap to add)
- Child's streak and progress visualization
- Teacher messages and quick-reply options
- "When Stuck on a Word" reference guide
- Badge/sticker collection showcase

## Section 5: Touch-Only UX Patterns

### 5.1 Core Principle: Minimize Typing

The primary goal is to eliminate keyboard typing entirely for the nightly logging workflow. Research shows that any requirement to type significantly reduces completion rates for habit-tracking apps.

### 5.2 Book Selection Interface

#### For Parents: Visual Book List

Display assigned books as large tappable cards showing cover image, title, and book type badge (green for Library, blue for Decodable). Since children typically have 2-4 books assigned at a time, this list is easily scannable and one-tap selectable.

#### For Teachers: ISBN Scanning + Search

Teachers benefit from barcode scanning when assigning books to students. Point camera at ISBN barcode to auto-populate all book details from database (Open Library API, Google Books API). Also provide search fallback for books without scannable barcodes.

### 5.3 Assessment Interfaces

#### Traffic Light System

Three large circular buttons in a row: Green ("I felt confident!"), Orange ("A little challenging"), Red ("This was hard"). Child taps one to select. Selected button shows checkmark.

#### Emoji Face Scale

Alternative to traffic lights: 4-5 emoji faces ranging from frustrated to excited. Research shows emoji scales produce equivalent assessment accuracy to numeric scales while being more engaging for children.

#### Star Rating

Interactive 4-5 star component. Child taps or swipes to select star count. Use both color AND shape changes for accessibility (not just color).

### 5.4 Pre-Written Comment Templates

Categories of tap-to-add comment chips:

- **Encouragement:** "Great reading tonight!" | "Loved hearing you read" | "Wonderful progress"
- **Reading Skills:** "Read with good expression" | "Sounded out new words well" | "Good finger tracking"
- **Engagement:** "Enjoyed discussing the story" | "Asked great questions" | "Wants to read more"
- **Challenges:** "Found some words tricky" | "Needed help with new words" | "Was tired tonight"

Parents can tap multiple chips to build a comment. Optional: voice-to-text for detailed custom comments.

### 5.5 Gesture Guidelines by Age

| Age Group | Recommended Gestures | Avoid |
|-----------|---------------------|-------|
| Ages 3-5 | Tap, swipe, grab | Pinch, rotate, multi-finger |
| Ages 6-8 | Tap, swipe, drag | Complex multi-finger |
| Touch Targets | Minimum 2cm x 2cm for ages under 8 | Small buttons, text links |

## Conclusions & Recommendations

This research demonstrates that Lumi has a significant opportunity to improve upon physical reading diaries while maintaining their proven educational value. As a **tracking and diary system** (not a digital reading platform), Lumi works alongside physical books rather than replacing them.

### Critical Success Factors

1. **Maintain the Library/Decodable Distinction:** This is pedagogically essential. Teachers pre-set book types when assigning.
2. **Teacher-Driven Book Assignment:** ISBN scanning benefits teachers assigning books, not parents logging reading.
3. **Minimal Parent Friction:** Parents select from short assigned book list - no scanning or typing needed.
4. **Preserve the Gamification Magic:** Streaks, milestones, and digital badges must feel as rewarding as physical stickers.
5. **Support Parent-Teacher Communication:** This is a unique value proposition of the reading diary format.

### Competitive Advantage

Unlike generic reading trackers (Goodreads, Beanstack), Lumi is specifically designed for the Australian early literacy market with built-in support for the science of reading and decodable text methodology. The teacher-assigns-books workflow mirrors actual classroom practice, making adoption seamless for schools already using physical reading diaries.
